Make at home

Save money. Control quality. Cleaner than store-bought. All from regular toned/full-fat milk and basic pantry items.

  • Curd / Dahi — 1 cup
    Boil 200ml full-fat milk, cool to body temp (warm but not hot to touch). Stir in 1 tsp starter curd, cover with lid. Leave at room temp 6–8 hrs (longer in winter). Refrigerate once set.
  • Greek yogurt / Hung curd — 120g
    Tie 200g regular curd in clean muslin/cotton cloth, hang over a bowl 2–3 hrs at room temp. The whey drips out, leaving thick creamy yogurt. Use straight in smoothie bowls and lassis.
  • Buttermilk / Chaas — 200ml
    Whisk 50g curd with 150ml chilled water till frothy. Add ½ tsp roasted jeera powder, pinch of black salt, 4–5 chopped curry leaves, coriander. Serve cold.
  • Paneer — 100g
    Boil 500ml full-fat milk. Once boiling, lower flame, add 1 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar, stir gently. Milk will split into curds + whey within 30 sec. Strain through muslin, rinse with cold water to remove sourness, gather edges and press flat under a heavy bowl for 30 min.
  • Sprouted moong — 100g
    Soak 50g green moong overnight in plenty of water. Drain, rinse, tie in slightly damp cloth. Leave in warm spot (kitchen counter) for 24–36 hrs. Done when 0.5–1 cm white sprouts appear. Refrigerate up to 3 days.
  • Sattu drink — 250ml
    Whisk 1.5 tbsp roasted sattu powder into 250ml chilled water till smooth and lumpless. Add juice of ½ lemon, pinch of black salt, ½ tsp roasted jeera powder, chopped mint. Serve immediately over ice.

Acidity & bloat protocol

Built for your active stomach issues. CCF tea is your secret weapon — drink it any time you feel off.

  • CCF tea — 1 tsp each jeera + coriander seeds + saunf boiled in 250ml water 5 min, strained. Drink hot. Single best digestive in your kitchen.
  • Hing in every tadka — already in most recipes. Reduces gas + bloat.
  • Avoid raw onion, raw garlic, raw cabbage — all trigger bloat for you
  • No ice-cold water with meals — room temp or warm only
  • Chew slowly — 20 chews per bite, especially for dal & sabzi
  • Walk 5–10 min after every main meal — non-negotiable
  • Stop eating 2.5 hrs before bed — lying down with food = acid reflux
  • Cook cruciferous (cauliflower, broccoli) well — never raw
  • One glass buttermilk daily for probiotics
  • Skip chana entirely — common acidity trigger, swapped to dal in this plan
  • Light masala always — heavy chili + garam masala = acid spike

Air fryer swaps

Most pan-grill recipes work better in your air fryer — less oil, more even, hands-off.

  • Paneer tikka — marinate 20 min, air-fry 180°C / 10 min, flip halfway
  • Tofu cubes — toss in ½ tsp oil + masala, 180°C / 12 min
  • Chicken breast — marinate 20 min, 200°C / 12 min flipping once
  • Roasted makhana — 160°C / 5 min, no oil needed
  • Soya chunks — boil, squeeze, toss with masala, 180°C / 8 min for crisp edges
  • Sweet potato slices — 200°C / 12 min as occasional cheat side
  • Chilla / dosa — pan-cook these, air fryer doesn't work for batters
  • Less oil = lighter on stomach + fewer calories per meal

Vrat flours compared

For Tuesday vrat days. All three are gluten-free, vrat-compliant, and roughly the same calories (~340 kcal/100g). Pick by protein, taste, and how easy they are to work with.

  • Rajgira (amaranth) — ★ Top pick. 14g protein/100g · beige · mild nutty · easiest to roll · gentlest on gut. Best for parathas, rotis, cheelas.
  • Kuttu (buckwheat) — 13g protein/100g · dark brown · earthy & slightly bitter · needs potato to bind · can feel heavy. Traditional choice.
  • Singhara (water chestnut) — Only 3g protein/100g (mostly starch) · pale white · mild sweet · forgiving and easy to roll · lightest texture. Best for tikkis and rotis.
  • Sabudana (tapioca/sago) — Not a flour, but soaked pearls. Pure starch (~0g protein) · use for khichdi, tikki, kheer. Pairs well with paneer for protein.
  • Samak rice (barnyard millet) — 7g protein/100g · grain-like, cooks like rice in 10 min · perfect pulao alternative.
  • Pro tip: Always combine vrat flours with paneer or curd for protein — alone they're carb-heavy.
